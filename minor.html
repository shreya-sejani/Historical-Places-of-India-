Taj mahal

The Taj Mahal, one of the seven wonders of the world, is situated in Agra along the Yamuna River. The Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan erected it as a monument to honour Mumtaz Mahal, his third wife. The grave of Shah Jahan himself is also located there. The Taj Mahal, which covers an area of around 42 acres, began construction in 1631 and took 17 years to complete in 1648! Taj Mahal, one of the most stunning structures in the world, was built in the 17th century using only white marble. Throughout the course of a year, more people than Agra's whole population pass through the beautiful gates to get a sight of this magnificent structure! The Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, signifies "Crown of Palaces" in Persian. The entrance features water channels and fountains, making the monument even more magnificent. This breathtaking spectacle's reflection in the Yamuna is nearly poetry in its perfection!

Agra Fort

The Agra Fort in Uttar Pradesh is one of India's oldest historical sites. The 16th century Agra Fort, a true example of beautiful Mughal architecture, was erected during Akbar's rule and is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It was the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638, when the capital was moved from Agra to Delhi. The fort's structure also includes numerous architectural types, as well as beautiful marble and sandstone artwork. It is located around 2.5 kilometres northwest of the Taj Mahal.

Red Fort

The Red Fort in Delhi is a must-see among India's well-known historical sites. The Red Fort, also known as the Lal Qila, was built in the 17th century under Shah Jahan's rule. The Red Fort was built with enormous red sandstone walls in the Mughal architectural style. Its features include its lovely geometric gardens, spectacular balconies, and rich entertainment halls. The complex's two most well-known buildings are the smaller Hall of Private Audience (Diwan-i-Khas), which features a pavilion made of white marble, and the larger Hall of Public Audience (Diwan-i-Am), which has 60 red sandstone pillars supporting a flat roof.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar in Delhi is one of India's top ten historical sites. The Qutub Minar, also known as the Victory Tower and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the tallest brick minaret in the entire world, rising tall at a height of 72.5 metres. The Qutub Minar is made of red sandstone and marble, and it features numerous exquisite sculptures. After Delhi's last Hindu emperor was defeated, the Qutub Minar was constructed in the late 1100s to commemorate Muslim dominance in the city. Qutab-ud-din Aibak started the building, and his successors Iltutmish and Firoz Shah Tughlak continued it.

Fatehpur Sikri

Fatehpur Sikri, a town in Uttar Pradesh's Agra district, is a gem of an architectural site and one of the most gorgeous historical locations in all of India. Fatehpur Sikri, a site that has been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, was established by the Mughal Emperor Akbar in the late 16th century and is primarily composed of red sandstone. The entire city of Fatehpur Sikri is fortified, and it currently features Jodha Bai's Palace, Buland Darwaza, Jama masjid, Ibadat Khana, the Tomb of Salim Chishti, among other attractions.

Hawa Mahal

Along with being a notable landmark of Rajasthan, the Hawa Mahal in Jaipur is one of the best historical locations in all of India. The 5-story Hawa Mahal, which dates to the 18th century, is a building that looks out over Jaipur's busy streets. Moreover, the Rajput, Mughal, and Islamic architectural styles have been combined to create it. The Hawa Mahal was built by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh in such a way that the tiny windows here allow winds to enter the palace and keep it cool during the sweltering months.

Amer Fort

Amer Fort in Jaipur is another of India's most well-known historical sites. This fort, one of the oldest in the nation, was built by Maharaja Man Singh in the sixteenth century. It boasts ornately carved walls, lavish ceilings, several courtyards, and magnificent entryways. The Amer Fort, a recognised UNESCO World Heritage Site, also has a museum, garden, restaurant, and other amenities. One of the fort's most notable features are its winding staircases and labyrinthine tunnels. In the past, a number of Rajput kings also used the Amer Fort as their primary residence.

India Gate

India Gate may be the most well-known historical site in the country. One of the largest war memorials in all of India, this sandstone-granite arch gate is a well-known feature of Delhi. It is surrounded by lush lawns and gardens. The India Gate, also called the All-India War Memorial, is a monument that stands along Rajpath and is sometimes compared to the Arc de Triomphe in France, the Gateway of India in Mumbai, and the Arch of Constantine in Rome. The 82,000 Indian and British soldiers who gave their lives in the First World War and the Third Anglo-Afghan War are honoured by this 42-meter-high memorial.

Golden Temple

The renowned Golden Temple, which is situated in the Punjab city of Amritsar, is another of the top 10 historical sites in India. The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the most significant Sikh sacred shrine and honours peace, tranquilly, and fraternity. The gurdwara, which is two stories tall, has a distinctive domed architecture, with the dome portion entirely covered in pure gold and the remainder in white marble. You can enjoy the daily Langar here in addition to witnessing significant sights like the Akal Takht, Sikh History Museum, Clock Tower, and Teja Singh Samundri Hall, which draw thousands of worshippers and tourists each day.

Ajanta Elora Caves

The Ajanta and Ellora Caves, which are close to Aurangabad in Maharashtra, are not just UNESCO World Heritage Sites but also India's greatest historical locations. These caves, which are filled with exquisite murals, paintings, sculptures, Buddhist monasteries, and Hindu and Jain temples, are said to represent some of the earliest examples of Buddhist construction. The Ellora Caves are more elaborate and are 34 in number, dating from between the 6th and 11th centuries, whilst the Ajanta Caves are 29 in number and come from between the 2nd and 6th centuries.
